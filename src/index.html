About Me

Me in my previous life

I used to be a chemist and now I'm a software developer. But why did I switch?

Well, I knew from my PhD that I love breaking down and tackling complex problems, as well as evaluating different problem-solving strategies and collaborating with others to share ideas.

The project management, problem-solving and interpersonal skills I'd developed felt like a natural fit for software development. The only problem was: I didn't know how to code. To fix that, I enrolled in a bootcamp at iO Academy and started learning the technical skills I needed.

Since then, I've built on these foundations through personal projects and now feel confident about applying my skills professionally. My research background means I can bring a different perspective, along with a natural eye for detail, and an openness to ask questions and get things wrong.

Now based in Copenhagen, I'm seeking a full-stack or back-end developer role, but I'm also open to internships that'll help me reach that goal, while allowing me to further develop my skills and contribute to a team.
